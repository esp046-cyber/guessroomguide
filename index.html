Guest Room Attendant Beginner's Guide

Room Setup

  • Change bed linens completely (sheets, pillowcases, duvet)
  • Replace towels (bath, hand, face)
  • Dust all surfaces including nightstands and TV
  • Vacuum carpets and mop bathroom floor
  • Empty trash bins and replace liners

Bathroom Standards

  • Scrub toilet, sink, and tub/shower thoroughly
  • Polish mirrors and chrome fixtures
  • Restock amenities (soap, shampoo, toilet paper)
  • Check for hair and stains
  • Ensure drain is clear

Final Touches

  • Arrange pillows neatly on bed
  • Set temperature to 72¬∞F (22¬∞C)
  • Close curtains halfway
  • Check all lights work
  • Place welcome card on desk

Step-by-Step Process

  • Step 1: Knock and announce "Housekeeping"
  • Step 2: Open curtains for natural light
  • Step 3: Strip bed and collect used towels
  • Step 4: Clean bathroom first (wet areas dry while you work)
  • Step 5: Dust high to low (ceiling fan, fixtures, furniture)
  • Step 6: Make bed with fresh linens
  • Step 7: Vacuum/mop floors last
  • Step 8: Final inspection walk-through

Time Management

  • Bedroom cleaning: 10-12 minutes
  • Bathroom cleaning: 8-10 minutes
  • Final touches: 2-3 minutes
  • Total target time: 20-25 minutes
  • Daily room quota: 14-16 rooms per shift

Pro Tips

  • Start from farthest point and work toward door
  • Top-to-bottom cleaning prevents re-work
  • Keep cart stocked and organized
  • Use microfiber cloths for streak-free surfaces
  • Always wear gloves when handling linens

Living Area

  • Vacuum all carpeted areas thoroughly
  • Dust coffee table, side tables, entertainment center
  • Arrange sofa cushions and throw pillows
  • Clean glass surfaces (windows, mirrors)
  • Restock minibar and check expiration dates

Bedroom Area

  • Premium linens with hospital corners
  • Extra pillows arranged decoratively
  • Turn-down service preparation
  • Check closet for hangers (minimum 10)
  • Ensure luggage rack is clean and stable

Kitchenette

  • Clean countertops and sink thoroughly
  • Wipe down microwave inside and out
  • Check refrigerator temperature and cleanliness
  • Restock coffee, tea, and utensils
  • Replace sponge and dishcloth

Suite Inspection Points

  • Check all doors and locks function properly
  • Test all light switches and dimmers
  • Verify AC/heating in all rooms
  • Inspect furniture for damage or stains
  • Ensure all TV remotes have batteries
  • Check balcony/patio is clean and safe

Time Breakdown

  • Living area: 15-20 minutes
  • Bedroom: 15-18 minutes
  • Bathrooms: 10-12 minutes each
  • Kitchenette: 8-10 minutes
  • Total target: 45-60 minutes

Pro Tips

  • Clean one room at a time completely
  • Pay attention to multiple bathrooms
  • Check behind doors and under furniture
  • Coordinate with turn-down service team
  • Report VIP arrivals to supervisor

Premium Standards

Spa Bathroom

Details Matter

White Glove Checklist

  • No dust visible anywhere (use flashlight test)
  • All items arranged symmetrically
  • Fresh scent throughout (subtle, not overpowering)
  • Temperature set to guest preference if known

Luxury Time Standards

  • Detail work: 10-15 minutes

Bed Making Technique

  • Start with mattress pad smooth and centered
  • Fitted sheet: start opposite corners, pull tight
  • Flat sheet: top edge even with mattress top
  • Hospital corners: 45-degree fold, tuck under
  • Duvet: shake to distribute filling evenly
  • Pillows: fluff and arrange standing against headboard

Beginner Tips

  • Always knock and announce before entering
  • Wear comfortable, non-slip shoes
  • Stay hydrated throughout your shift
  • Ask questions - experienced staff want to help
  • Take photos of perfect rooms for reference

Chemical Safety

  • NEVER mix bleach with ammonia (toxic gas)
  • Always wear gloves when handling chemicals
  • Read product labels and follow instructions
  • Use proper ventilation when cleaning
  • Store chemicals in original containers only
  • Keep safety data sheets (SDS) accessible

Physical Safety

  • Lift with legs, not back (bend at knees)
  • Ask for help with heavy items over 25 lbs
  • Use carts to transport supplies, don't carry
  • Wear anti-slip shoes on wet floors
  • Place "wet floor" signs immediately
  • Report spills and hazards right away

Guest Property

  • Never touch guest belongings unnecessarily
  • Don't move valuables or documents
  • Report found items to supervisor immediately
  • Knock multiple times before entering with key
  • Respect "Do Not Disturb" signs always
  • If guest is present, ask permission to clean

Emergency Procedures

  • Know location of fire extinguishers
  • Memorize emergency exit routes
  • Report suspicious items or behavior
  • Never enter room with safety concerns
  • Call security for any threatening situation
  • Know first aid station location

Injury Prevention

  • Stretch before starting shift
  • Take scheduled breaks to rest
  • Alternate tasks to avoid repetitive strain
  • Use proper posture when making beds
  • Report pain or discomfort early
  • Use ergonomic tools when available

Safety First

  • Your safety is more important than speed
  • Report ALL injuries, even minor ones
  • Follow hotel safety protocols exactly
  • Never take shortcuts with safety
  • Attend all safety training sessions

Common Stains

  • Coffee/Tea: Blot, cold water, enzyme cleaner
  • Wine: Salt or club soda immediately, then wash
  • Blood: Cold water only (hot sets stain)
  • Ink: Rubbing alcohol on cotton ball, blot
  • Makeup: Dish soap and warm water
  • Grease: Cornstarch to absorb, then detergent

Carpet Stains

  • Act fast - fresh stains easier to remove
  • Blot, never rub (rubbing spreads stain)
  • Work from outside edge toward center
  • Use white cloths (colored cloths may bleed)
  • Test cleaner on hidden area first
  • Extract moisture with dry towels after cleaning

Bathroom Stains

  • Hard water: Vinegar solution, let sit 15 minutes
  • Soap scum: Baking soda paste, scrub gently
  • Mildew: Bleach solution (well-ventilated area)
  • Rust: Commercial rust remover only
  • Toilet ring: Pumice stone (porcelain only)
  • Grout: Hydrogen peroxide and baking soda

Stain Removal Steps

  • Step 1: Identify stain type if possible
  • Step 2: Remove excess (scrape or blot)
  • Step 3: Pre-treat with appropriate solution
  • Step 4: Let solution sit (5-15 minutes)
  • Step 5: Blot or scrub gently
  • Step 6: Rinse with clean water
  • Step 7: Dry thoroughly
  • Step 8: Repeat if necessary

What to Report

  • Stains that won't come out after 2 attempts
  • Burns or permanent damage to surfaces
  • Large or unusual stains (investigate first)
  • Bodily fluid stains (biohazard protocol)
  • Chemical spills or unknown substances
  • Any damage to hotel property

Pro Tips

  • Cold water for protein stains (blood, egg)
  • Hot water for grease and oil stains
  • Always test cleaners in hidden spots first
  • Keep stain guide card with supplies
  • Document damage with photos if needed

Guest Interaction

  • Always greet guests with smile and eye contact
  • Identify yourself: "Good morning, I'm housekeeping"
  • Ask preferred cleaning time if guest is present
  • Offer assistance: "Is there anything you need?"
  • Respect guest privacy - don't ask personal questions
  • Keep interactions brief and professional

Communication Skills

  • Learn basic phrases in common guest languages
  • Speak clearly and at moderate pace
  • Use positive body language (open posture)
  • Listen actively to guest requests
  • Repeat requests to confirm understanding
  • Write down complex requests immediately

Handling Requests

  • Extra towels: Deliver within 15 minutes
  • Extra pillows/blankets: From linen storage
  • Toiletries: Stock from housekeeping cart
  • Ice bucket: Fill and return promptly
  • Room service tray removal: Clear immediately

Professional Etiquette

  • Name badge visible and polished

  • No strong perfume or cologne
  • Clean, trimmed nails

Difficult Situations

  • Complaints: Listen calmly, apologize, inform supervisor
  • Angry guests: Stay calm, don't argue, get manager
  • Missing items: Report immediately, file incident report
  • Inappropriate behavior: Leave room, call security
  • Language barrier: Use simple words, gestures, translator app
  • Refusal to leave: Don't force, contact front desk

Excellence Tips

  • Anticipate needs (extra towels for families)

  • Close laptop/tablet if open (gently)

Turndown Timing

  • Suite: 8-12 minutes

Quality vs Speed

  • Speed comes naturally with practice
  • Never sacrifice quality for speed
  • Failed inspection = do it again (wastes time)
  • Consistent routine increases efficiency
  • Master basics before trying shortcuts
  • Ask fast colleagues for their methods

Pro Efficiency Hacks

  • Spray bathroom while stripping bed (multi-task)
  • Use microfiber for faster, better cleaning
  • Carry small kit for quick stayover refreshes
  • Position cart to minimize walking
  • Learn hotel layout to plan optimal route

Maintenance Issues

  • Leaking faucets or toilet - report immediately
  • Broken AC/heating - high priority
  • Electrical problems - do not touch, call maintenance
  • Clogged drains - use plunger first, then report
  • Damaged furniture - document with photos
  • Pest sightings - isolate room, notify management

Bed Bugs Protocol

  • Signs: tiny brown bugs, blood spots on sheets
  • DO NOT clean the room further
  • Notify supervisor immediately
  • Seal room and mark as out of service
  • Do not move items from room to cart
  • Follow hotel's pest control procedure

Lost and Found

  • Check all areas: under bed, closet, safe
  • Look in drawers, behind curtains, bathroom
  • Document item: description, location, date/time
  • Tag item with room number and your name
  • Turn in to lost and found immediately
  • Never keep items, even if seemingly worthless

Problem Solving Steps

  • Step 1: Identify the problem clearly
  • Step 2: Determine if you can fix it
  • Step 3: If yes, fix it properly
  • Step 4: If no, document problem details
  • Step 5: Report to appropriate department
  • Step 6: Follow up if urgent
  • Step 7: Mark room status correctly
  • Step 8: Update supervisor on resolution

Guest Complaints

  • Listen without interrupting
  • Apologize sincerely: "I'm sorry for the inconvenience"
  • Don't make excuses or blame others
  • Offer immediate solution if possible
  • Get supervisor if you can't resolve
  • Follow up to ensure satisfaction

Common Issues & Solutions

  • No hot water: Check if problem is building-wide
  • TV not working: Check power, batteries, connections
  • Key card issues: Send guest to front desk
  • Noisy room: Offer to move guest if available
  • Dirty room complaint: Re-clean immediately
  • Missing amenities: Deliver personally with apology

Prevention Tips

  • Double-check room before marking complete
  • Test all lights, AC, TV during cleaning
  • Report potential problems before guest finds them
  • Keep supervisor informed of recurring issues
  • Learn from mistakes - don't repeat them
